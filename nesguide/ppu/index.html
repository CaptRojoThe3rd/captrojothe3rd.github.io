The PPU, or Picture Processing Unit, is the graphics chip of the NES. It's responsible for displaying a game's graphics on the screen.






The PPU has two ways of rendering things on the screen: the background and the sprites. The background is a mostly stationary layer meant for, well, backgrounds of things. It is made up of tiles that can't move independently of each other. The sprites are small tiles that can move independently of each other - these are useful for things that move a lot, like characters or moving platforms.
